Offers

How do offers work?

Offers are for when you love an item, but don’t love the price. After an item has been on the app for 72 hours and it hasn’t sold, offers will open up on that item. You’ll be able to enter a price lower than the listed price, and if the owner accepts, the order will go through and you’ll be charged.

Do offers expire?

Sellers have 24 hours to respond to an offer before it expires. You can’t cancel an offer after it’s been submitted -- offers are binding.

Is there a minimum offer amount?

To protect sellers from lowball offers, we enforce a minimum offer. If your offer is lower than we allow, we'll show an error message when you tap to make the offer.

Why do some items not have a button to make an offer?

Offers aren’t available on an item until it’s been posted for 72 hours. This is to give sellers a few days for their item to sell at full price before they need to consider any offers. If an item doesn’t have a Make Offer button, offers haven’t opened up yet on that item.

How do I leave an offer?

If an item has been listed for 72 hours and still hasn’t sold, there will be a “Make Offer” button next to the “Buy Now” button. Simply tap the button, enter your offer, and then select your shipping and payment preferences. You won’t be charged unless the owner approves your offer.

Do I need to pay extra for shipping?

Nope! All prices on Curtsy include shipping. For instance, if an item is listed at $50 and you offer $42, you'll only be charged $42 if your item is accepted.

What if my offer isn’t accepted?

Oh no! We recommend messaging the seller and trying to agree on a price. If they’re willing to accept a lower offer, you can make another offer at that lower price.

Do I have to do anything after my offer is accepted?

Nope! Your card will be charged and the item will be shipping to you shortly. Feel free to message the seller and ask when they can ship it.

What if I change my mind?

Offers are binding, meaning if the seller accepts, you’re agreeing to purchase the item from them and your card will be charged. Be sure that you want the item before you make an offer.
